Precursor: Introduction of MMC

1997

In 1997, the MultiMediaCard (MMC) was introduced as a compact flash memory solution for portable electronics. Though not an SD card itself, the MMC laid the groundwork for the evolution of flash memory storage by demonstrating the potential for small, non-volatile storage in consumer devices.

Founding of the SD Association

2000

In 2000, leading companies including SanDisk, Panasonic, and Toshiba established the SD Association. This consortium standardized the Secure Digital (SD) format, ensuring interoperability among devices and providing a unified specification that accelerated the adoption of SD cards in consumer electronics.

Release of the First SD Cards

2000

Also in 2000, following the establishment of the standard, the first SD cards were released. These early cards supported capacities of up to 2 GB and were primarily used in digital cameras and portable devices, marking a significant improvement over previous storage media in terms of capacity, reliability, and ease of use.

Introduction of the microSD Card

2005

In 2005, the microSD card was introduced as a smaller form factor of the standard SD card. Retaining the performance and reliability of its larger counterpart, the microSD quickly became integral to the design of mobile phones, MP3 players, and other portable electronics, spurring rapid growth in mobile computing.

Ratification of the SDHC Standard

2006

In 2006, the SD Association ratified the SDHC (Secure Digital High Capacity) standard, expanding the maximum capacity of SD cards from 2 GB to 32 GB and introducing the FAT32 file system. This standard enabled devices to capture higher-resolution images and record longer videos, addressing the increasing demands of digital photography and multimedia applications.

Ratification of the SDXC Standard

2009

The SDXC (Secure Digital eXtended Capacity) standard was introduced in 2009, further extending the maximum storage capacity up to 2 TB and incorporating support for the exFAT file system. This development catered to high-end professional applications and advanced consumer devices, cementing the SD card's role as a versatile and scalable storage solution.
