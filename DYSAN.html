Formation of Dysan

1973

Dysan was founded in 1973 in San Jose, California by C. Norman Dion. From its inception, Dysan set out to become a leader in storage media manufacturing.

Development of the 5.25" Floppy Disk

1976

Dysan played an instrumental role in developing the 5.25" floppy disk, a format that revolutionized removable storage by providing a practical balance of capacity and portability.

Corporate Success and Recognition

1983

By 1983, Dysan had become a Fortune 500 company with over 1,200 employees and was ranked among the top ten private sector employers in Silicon Valley. Their Santa Clara facilities, known for innovative architectural features like expansive glass atriums and indoor landscaping, underscored the company's prominence.

Sale to Xidex Magnetics

Spring 1984

In the spring of 1984, Norman Dion sold Dysan to Xidex Magnetics, marking the beginning of a period of significant corporate change.
