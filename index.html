Pre-IBM

1890

Herman Hollerith develops the first punched card system for the U.S. Census, revolutionizing data processing.

IBM Standardization and Expansion

1928

IBM introduces the 80-column punch card, setting the industry standard for business, government, and scientific computing.

Peak Usage and Early Computing

1940s

IBM punch cards play a crucial role in World War II, used for logistics, codebreaking, and even the Manhattan Project.

Punch cards decline with the rise of magnetic storage, computer terminals, and floppy disks, but remain in use for early programming.

Discontinuation and Legacy

1984

IBM ceases production of punch card equipment, marking the end of their mainstream use. However, its legacy remains in the digital age.
