Early Concept

1725

Basile Bouchon develops a loom that uses perforated paper tape to control textile patterns, introducing an early form of programmable media.

Telegraph Communication

1846

Alexander Bain invents paper tape as a method for pre-recording telegraph messages, allowing faster and more reliable transmission of information.

Industrial Adoption

1930s

Paper tape gains widespread use in industries, particularly in teletype machines and automated message transmission, enabling more efficient data processing.

World War II and Early Computing

1940s

Paper tape is used in the British Colossus machine to decrypt enemy communications, demonstrating its importance in early computing and codebreaking.
